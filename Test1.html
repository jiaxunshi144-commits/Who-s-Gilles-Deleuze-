Gilles Deleuze

French Philosopher and Influential Thinker (1925 - 1995)

Biography

Gilles Deleuze was a French philosopher who made significant contributions to philosophy, literature, film, and art theory. Born in 1925 in Paris, he studied philosophy at the Sorbonne and later became a professor at several universities.

Deleuze is best known for his works exploring metaphysics, epistemology, and contemporary philosophy, often in collaboration with psychoanalyst Félix Guattari. His philosophy is marked by a rejection of traditional structures and an emphasis on difference, multiplicity, and desire.

Main Concepts

Difference and Repetition: Deleuze challenges the primacy of identity in philosophy and explores the significance of difference in shaping reality.

Rhizome: Developed with Guattari, the rhizome is a model of knowledge and society emphasizing non-hierarchical connections and multiplicity.

Assemblage Theory: This concept involves understanding entities as composites of heterogeneous components forming functional wholes.

Notable Works

  • Difference and Repetition (1968)
  • Anti-Oedipus (1972, with Félix Guattari)
  • A Thousand Plateaus (1980, with Félix Guattari)
  • What is Philosophy? (1991, with Félix Guattari)

Legacy

Deleuze's philosophy has influenced fields beyond philosophy including cultural studies, political theory, art, film studies, and critical theory. His radical ideas continue to inspire new ways of thinking about difference, power, and social organization.
